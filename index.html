My name is Dola Saha. I'm from Bangladesh. In my country, peoples like to eat sweets. My favourite is Roshogolla.This dessert is so soft that it blends into the mouth. It is made from curdled milk. Today I want to tell you how to make roshgolla.

For making roshogolla we need:

Ingredients for roshogolla

  • 1 litre milk or four cups(full fat or whole milk)
  • 2 to 3 tablespoon lemon juice üçãüçã
  • 1 litre ice water or few ice cubes üßäüßä

To make suger syrup

  • 1.5 cups refine white sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 3 green cardamom pods
  • Half teaspoon rosewater (optional)
  • 1 pinch saffron stand (optional)

How to make the recipe:-

Making chena for roshogolla

  1. Bring milk to boil in a pot.
  2. Add 2tbsp lemon juice and stir until the milk curdles.
  3. When you see the milk curdled completely, switch of the stove. Rest for 2mins.
  4. Then pour cold water to the pot. This will stop the chena from cooking further & keeps it soft.
  5. Place a colander over a large bowl & line with a thin cloth. Drain the curdled milk.
  6. Rinse the chena under running water to remove it's acidic flavor.
  7. Tie the cloth.Squeeze it well to remove the excess whey as much as possible
  8. Hang out for 1 hour. Chena should not have any excess whey. It must be crumbly.

Making sugar syrup

  1. Add sugar, cardamom and water in a wide pan or pot.
  2. Stir to dissolve the sugar. Bring it to a boil.☀

How to make roshogolla

  1. Knead the chena well to make it smooth dough for 3 to 5 mins
  2. Do not over knead to the extent that the chena turns greasy or soggy.
  3. When you see the mixture turns uniformly smooth and no more grainy, then stop kneading.
  4. Take small portion of this and roll to tiny balls
  5. They should not be tiny and not big as they expend in size after boilling. I made about 16 balls.‚ò∫Ô∏è
  6. Bring the sugar syrup to a rolling boil on medium flame.
  7. Remove the cardamom pods and add the balls one after the other gently.
  8. Cover the pot immidiately with a lid.
  9. The syrup must be boiling steadily at a constant heat.
  10. Cook on a medium flame for 9 to 10 mins. The syrup must be bubbling and boling steadily through out this time. So adjust the heat as needed depending on the kind of pot and stove used. üç≥üç≥
  11. After 5 mins gently stir the sugar syrup with a skewer without touching the roshogollas. Cover immidiately.ü•Ñü•Ñ
  12. This ensur even cooking and puffing. During the cooking time if you feel the heat is so much reduce slightly, but ensure it is still bobbluing and boiling.
  13. After 10 mins remove the pot immidiately from the stove to prevent roshogolla from cooking further.
  14. Keep the lid closed always otherwise they will shrink or fall flat. Do not open at least for 20 mins.
  15. They doudle in size and also sink in syrup when cooked completely.
  16. Allow roshogolla to rest and cool completely. Serve them chilled. If desired garnish with saffron. üòçüòç
